LIVELIHOOD PROGRAM

Skill Training and Placement Support for Underprivileged Youth

Youth unemployment and underemployment are major challenges facing rural India. Many young people lack the skills, guidance, and opportunities needed to secure decent employment. Gandhiji Seva Sangam's Livelihood Program addresses this critical gap by providing comprehensive skill training, career counseling, and placement support to underprivileged youth, enabling them to break the cycle of poverty and build sustainable livelihoods.

Our Mission

To empower rural youth with market-relevant skills, entrepreneurial capabilities, and employment opportunities that lead to economic independence and improved quality of life.

Program Components

1. Vocational Skills Training

We offer industry-aligned training programs in high-demand sectors:

  • Information Technology: Basic computer skills, MS Office, data entry, hardware maintenance, and web design
  • Hospitality & Tourism: Front office operations, housekeeping, food & beverage service, and hotel management
  • Retail & Sales: Customer service, merchandising, inventory management, and sales techniques
  • Healthcare & Nursing: Basic healthcare assistant training, geriatric care, and patient care skills
  • Automotive: Two-wheeler and four-wheeler repair, maintenance, and servicing
  • Construction: Plumbing, electrical work, masonry, painting, and carpentry
  • Beauty & Wellness: Professional beautician courses, spa therapy, and salon management
  • Tailoring & Fashion: Garment construction, embroidery, pattern making, and fashion technology

2. Soft Skills & Personality Development

Complementing technical training with essential life skills:

  • Communication Skills: English speaking, presentation skills, and effective communication
  • Workplace Etiquette: Professional behavior, time management, and workplace ethics
  • Interview Preparation: Resume writing, mock interviews, and confidence building
  • Digital Literacy: Email, internet navigation, online applications, and digital payments
  • Financial Literacy: Budgeting, savings, banking, and managing personal finances
  • Problem-Solving: Critical thinking, decision-making, and analytical skills

3. Entrepreneurship Development

Supporting youth who aspire to start their own businesses:

  • Business Planning: Identifying business opportunities, market research, and business plan development
  • Financial Management: Bookkeeping, cost calculations, pricing strategies, and profit management
  • Marketing Skills: Product promotion, customer acquisition, and digital marketing basics
  • Seed Capital Support: Access to microloans and connections with financial institutions
  • Mentorship: Ongoing guidance from successful entrepreneurs and business advisors
  • Market Linkages: Connecting youth enterprises with buyers, suppliers, and government schemes

4. Career Counseling & Guidance

Helping youth make informed career decisions:

  • Aptitude Assessment: Understanding individual strengths, interests, and career potential
  • Career Path Mapping: Exploring various career options and educational pathways
  • Higher Education Guidance: Information on colleges, courses, scholarships, and admission processes
  • Government Scheme Awareness: Connecting youth with skill development programs, apprenticeships, and subsidies
  • One-on-One Counseling: Personalized career guidance and problem-solving sessions

5. Placement & Employment Support

Bridging the gap between trained youth and employment opportunities:

  • Job Fairs: Organizing recruitment drives with partner companies across sectors
  • Industry Partnerships: Collaborations with employers for direct placements and internships
  • Campus Interviews: Facilitating on-site interviews at training centers
  • Job Portal: Maintaining database of job openings and connecting youth with opportunities
  • Follow-up Support: Post-placement monitoring and support to ensure job retention
  • Migration Support: Assistance for youth relocating to cities for employment, including accommodation guidance

6. Agriculture & Allied Livelihood

Promoting sustainable livelihoods in agriculture:

  • Modern Farming Techniques: Training in organic farming, precision agriculture, and crop diversification
  • Agri-entrepreneurship: Food processing, value addition, and agri-business management
  • Livestock Management: Dairy farming, poultry rearing, and goat/sheep farming
  • Fisheries & Aquaculture: Fish farming and pond management techniques
  • Market Linkages: Connecting farmers with wholesale markets, FPOs, and direct buyers

Our Impact

Our Livelihood Program has transformed countless lives:

  • Trained over 2,000 youth in various vocational skills over the past five years
  • Achieved 75% placement rate with average starting salary of ₹12,000 per month
  • Supported 150+ youth entrepreneurs in establishing micro-enterprises
  • Partnered with 85 companies across IT, hospitality, retail, and manufacturing sectors
  • Conducted 50+ job fairs connecting youth with over 200 employers
  • Provided career counseling to 3,500+ students from rural schools and colleges
  • Average income increase of 250% post-training compared to pre-training employment
  • Trained 400+ farmers in modern agricultural techniques and organic farming

Success Story

"Rajesh, a 22-year-old school dropout from a farmer family, had limited prospects beyond manual labor. He enrolled in our automotive repair training program and excelled in the six-month course. Through our placement cell, he secured a job at a reputed automobile service center in Madurai with a monthly salary of ₹15,000. Within two years, Rajesh saved enough to open his own motorcycle repair shop in his village. He now employs two other youth and earns ₹30,000 per month. His success has inspired ten more youth from his village to join our training programs."

Training Methodology

Our programs follow a comprehensive approach:

  • Duration: Courses ranging from 3 to 12 months depending on skill complexity
  • Practical Focus: 70% hands-on training with real equipment and workplace simulations
  • Industry Exposure: Visits to companies, guest lectures by industry experts, and internships
  • Certification: Recognized certificates from National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) and sector skill councils
  • Flexible Timings: Day and evening batches to accommodate students and working youth
  • Free Training: No fees for economically disadvantaged candidates
  • Stipend Support: Travel and food allowances for participants from extremely poor families

Partner Network

We collaborate with various stakeholders:

  • National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC): Funding and certification support
  • Industry Partners: Over 85 companies for training, internships, and placements
  • Training Partners: Technical institutes for specialized training programs
  • Government Schemes: DDU-GKY, PMKVY, and state skill development missions
  • Banks & Financial Institutions: Loans for aspiring entrepreneurs
  • Educational Institutions: Colleges for advanced training and degree programs

Future Plans

  • Launching online training programs to reach youth in remote areas
  • Starting apprenticeship programs in partnership with 50 industries
  • Creating an alumni network for mentorship and job referrals
  • Developing specialized programs for persons with disabilities
  • Establishing incubation centers for youth-led startups
  • Targeting 5,000 youth for skill training and placement by 2028
